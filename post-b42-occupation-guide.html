Build 42 Complete Occupation Guide: Best Traits, Playstyles & Tips

Written for Build 42 2026-05-11 ~8 min read

Choosing the right occupation and traits in Build 42 is the foundation of a successful run. With 8 points to spend, every decision matters. This guide covers every occupation, the best trait combinations, and hidden synergies that most players miss.

Best Occupations for Beginners

Fire Officer ?The Balanced Starter

Starting Skills: Fitness 4, Strength 4, Axe 3, Sprinting 2

Why it works: Excellent starting combat stats, axe skill means you can clear zombies efficiently from day one. The fitness and strength boost means you carry more and fight longer.

Recommended Traits:

  • Fast Healer (+4) ?Recover from wounds faster
  • Desensitized (+2) ?Less panic when surrounded
  • Light Pack (+1) ?Carry more with less penalty
  • Prone to Illness (?2) ?Acceptable trade for more points
  • Smoker (?2) ?Only if you can find cigarettes; otherwise avoid

Total Points: 8 (Fire Officer costs 0) + 5 (traits) = 13 points, minus 4 for negative traits = 9 usable points

Best For: First-time players who want combat effectiveness and survivability

Police Officer ?The Crowd Control Expert

Starting Skills: Fitness 3, Strength 3, Short Blunt 3, Sprinting 2

Why it works: Baton skill is underrated ?bats and pipes are everywhere, and Short Blunt is one of the fastest weapon classes. You'll clear rooms quickly without exhausting yourself.

Recommended Traits:

  • Dextrous (+2) ?Loot faster, critical for efficiency
  • Fast Learner (+3) ?XP bonus on everything
  • Wakeful (+1) ?Need less sleep
  • Asthmatic (?2) ?Stamina penalty, but manageable with Fitness
  • Weak Stomach (?1) ?Slight food penalty, worth the points

Best For: Players who want fast leveling and efficient looting

Advanced Occupations

Carpenter ?The Base Builder

Starting Skills: Carpentry 3, Maintenance 2

Why it works: If your goal is to build a fortress, start here. Carpentry 3 means you can build walls and reinforced doors immediately. Maintenance skill means your tools last longer.

Recommended Traits:

  • Handy (+2) ?Repair and build faster
  • Maintenance (+1) ?Tool durability
  • Thrifty (+2) ?Materials last longer
  • Unfit (?3) ?Acceptable if you're staying in base
  • Slow Reader (?1) ?Skill books take longer, but Carpentry is hands-on

Best For: Players who prioritize base building over exploration

Paramedic ?The Medic

Starting Skills: First Aid 5, Doctor 1

Why it works: First Aid 5 is massive. You can stitch wounds, treat infections, and use advanced medical supplies from day one. In multiplayer, you're invaluable.

Recommended Traits:

  • Fast Healer (+4) ?Stacks with First Aid for incredible recovery
  • Resilient (+2) ?Less likely to get sick
  • Desensitized (+2) ?Calm in high-stress situations
  • High Thirst (?1) ?Manageable with water stockpiles
  • Slow Healer (?2) ?Contradicts Fast Healer, but net positive for points

Best For: Multiplayer medic role or solo players who want maximum survivability

Point Optimization ?The Math Behind Good Builds

Build 42 uses a point system where occupations cost points (or give them back) and traits adjust the total. The goal is to maximize positive traits while minimizing negative ones that won't kill you.

The Golden Rule: Never take a negative trait that kills you faster than the positive traits save you. For example, "Obese" slows you down permanently ?that's a death sentence in a chase. "Underweight" is manageable with food planning.

Hidden Synergies Most Players Miss

Synergy 1: Dextrous + Lightfooted = Silent Looting

If you combine Dextrous (fast looting) with Lightfooted (quiet movement), you can loot buildings without attracting zombies. This is the most underrated combination in the game.

Synergy 2: Fast Learner + Reader = XP Machine

Fast Learner multiplies all XP, including from skill books. Reader speeds up reading. Together, you'll level through books twice as fast as normal.

Synergy 3: Carpenter + Maintenance + Thrifty = Infinite Building

These three traits mean you build faster, tools last longer, and materials aren't wasted. If you're building a large base, this combination saves hundreds of in-game hours.

What Not to Do

  • Don't stack too many negative traits: 3+ negative traits will cripple your character. Stick to 2.
  • Don't ignore Fitness and Strength: Even if you're a builder or medic, you need baseline survivability.
  • Don't pick "Unfit" and "Obese" together: This combination makes running impossible, and running is how you survive.
  • Don't over-plan: Traits matter, but your decisions in-game matter more. A well-played average build beats a poorly played optimized one.
