10 Things I Died to in My First Week of Project Zomboid (And How to Avoid Them)

Written for Build 42 2026-05-11 ~7 min read

This is how I died. And died again. And then a few more times. Here are the 10 mistakes that killed me most in my first week of Project Zomboid, with exactly what I should have done instead.

1. I Didn't Check What Building I Spawned In

I just ran outside immediately after spawning. Big mistake. The first house you're in has free loot if you look.

What I should have done: Press ESC, check my surroundings. If I'm in a residential house, grab the kitchen knife on the counter, fill a water bottle at the sink, take any bag I can find. That takes 30 seconds and gives you everything you need for hour one.

2. I Ran Everywhere and Ran Out of Stamina

I saw a zombie, panicked, and started sprinting. Ten seconds later my stamina bar was empty, I was moving slower than the zombie, and I had no fight left.

What I should have done: Walk normally to save stamina. Use the C key to crouch-walk when zombies are nearby ?they literally can't hear you if you're crouching on carpet or grass. Only sprint when there's genuinely no way around a zombie. And if your stamina drops below 50%, stop and rest. Walking with zero stamina means you die.

3. I Fought Three Zombies at Once

I thought I was doing fine against one zombie, then two showed up, then a third, and suddenly I was surrounded. That's how most of my deaths happened.

The rule I learned: One zombie = fight. Two zombies = risky, but you can use the shove-and-backpedal technique (Q key to shove, they can trip over each other). Three or more = run. Always. In Build 42 the new grapple mechanic means getting surrounded is basically instant death.

4. I Didn't Board Windows Before Night

I found a nice house, put my stuff down, went to sleep, and woke up to three zombies walking through my unboarded windows.

What I should have done: Find a hammer and nails (check garages, toolboxes, hardware stores). Board every ground-floor window before dark. Each window takes about 2 planks and 2 nails. Block the front door with a chair or sofa. If you don't have nails, tear apart a wooden fence with your hands ?desperate times.

5. I Didn't Watch the Weather Channel on TV

I got caught in rain with no rain jacket. My character got soaked, then cold, then sick. Getting sick without medicine is a death spiral.

What I should have done: Grab any TV you find (they're in almost every house and store). Plug it in or add batteries. Tune to Life and Living channel ?the weather forecast airs at 6:00 AM, 12:00 PM, and 6:00 PM. If rain is coming, find a rain jacket before heading out. If a cold front is predicted, grab warm clothes. The TV is free and it saves runs.

6. I Didn't Read Skill Books Before Leveling

I spent days practicing Carpentry by hammering windows, when I could have read a Carpentry book and gotten a huge XP boost instantly.

What I should have done: Grab every skill book you find. Read them FIRST, then practice the skill. The book gives a large one-time XP boost that multiplies all your hands-on practice after. I wasted a full week of in-game time because I didn't know this.

7. I Filled My Backpack Past 80% Weight

I grabbed everything I saw ?extra cans, random clothes, tools I didn't need. My carry weight hit 80% and my sprint speed dropped by half. A single zombie caught me because I couldn't run.

What I should have done: Watch the carry weight indicator. Priority: food/water > weapon > bag > tools > everything else. If something pushes you over 70%, drop it. You can always come back for it when you're stronger.

8. I Didn't Disinfect a Zombie Scratch

I got scratched by a zombie, figured it was just a scratch, and ignored it. A few hours later I was queasy. I didn't know if it was a 7% infection or not. Turned out it wasn't ?but I wasted my last hours panicking anyway.

What I should have done: Treat every zombie wound. Bandage it, disinfect it with disinfectant or alcohol bottles, then wait 6?2 hours. If the Queasy moodle shows up with no other cause (no rotten food, no corpse pile), it's Knox Infection and you're done. At least you'll know instead of wondering.

9. I Went Outside Without Checking the Temperature

I walked out in a t-shirt when it was 2°C. My character started shivering, body temperature dropped, and by the time I got back inside I had the beginnings of a cold.

What I should have done: Press Tab and check the weather panel. If it's below 10°C, wear at least a jacket and long pants. Below 0°C, you need a full winter coat, hat, and gloves. Check the TV weather forecast ?it tells you if temperatures are dropping.

10. I Didn't Have an Escape Plan

I set up my base on the second floor and removed the stairs (smart). But I didn't have sheet ropes ready. When a zombie somehow got inside through an unboarded window, I was trapped upstairs with nowhere to go.

What I should have done: Always keep sheet rope or a crafting recipe (3x sheets/blankets = sheet rope) ready. If things go wrong, right-click a window and use sheet rope to escape. I died because I treated my base like a fortress instead of a temporary shelter.

The Biggest Lesson I Learned

Week one isn't about being strong. It's about not making stupid mistakes. Every single death I had came down to impatience or ignorance. Slow down, check the weather, watch TV, and don't fight more zombies than you need to. Live today, tomorrow you'll know more.

This guide was put together by players who've died to learn this stuff so you don't have to. Updated as the game changes.
