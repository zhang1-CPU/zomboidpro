Best Melee Weapons for Beginners in Build 42 ?What to Grab First

Written for Build 42 2026-05-11 ~7 min read

A good weapon is the difference between walking away from a fight and not walking away at all. But "best" doesn't always mean "what you should start with." Here's what to pick up at each stage of your run.

Day One ?Grab Whatever Works

You just spawned in. Walk into the nearest house and check the kitchen and garage:

  • Kitchen Knife ?Every house has one. Fast swing speed, kills a couple zombies before it degrades. Don't try to tank a crowd with it though.
  • Golf Club ?Check living rooms and garages. Slightly longer reach than a knife, but durability is mediocre. Transitional weapon.
  • Hammer or Wrench ?Found in toolboxes. Damage isn't great, but they last forever and you'll need them for building anyway.

Common mistake: skipping these and hunting for "better" weapons. Early on, you don't have the Long Blunt or Axe skill to swing big weapons effectively ?they'll drain your stamina before you land a second hit.

Days 2? ?Time to Upgrade

Once you've survived the first couple nights, head to a hardware store or fire station:

Crowbar ?The All-Rounder

Hardware stores, garages, construction sites. Damage is above average, durability is solid, and you can pry open locked doors and windows with it. One crowbar will carry you further than you'd expect.

Note: it does benefit from Long Blade skill, so it might feel slow at first. Once that skill ticks up a couple levels, it hits noticeably harder.

Baseball Bat ?Early Game Workhorse

Sporting goods stores, schools, some garages. Once you grab one, find a hammer and nails and right-click the bat to "Reinforce with Nails." The damage jump is real ?a nailed bat will carry you well into midgame.

Downsides: takes up inventory space and is heavy. Don't carry more than one.

Fire Axe ?Save It for When You Need It

Fire stations (second floor, mounted on the wall), some garages. Top-tier damage ?takes out most zombies in one or two swings.

But it's heavy, drains stamina fast, and has a slow swing speed. Without Axe skill, you'll be gasping after three swings. Keep it as a backup for when a crowd gets too close, not as your primary weapon.

Midgame Picks (Two Weeks In)

You've got skills now. Here's what to look for long-term:

  • Machete ?Army surplus stores or police stations. High damage, great durability, excellent with Long Blade skill. Rare to find though.
  • Scythe ?Farms and barns. Huge reach, good for clearing small groups, but slow swing speed. Needs good positioning.
  • Crafted Spear ?Combine a wooden stick with a knife or fork using duct tape. Right-click the stick ?Combine ?select materials. Longest reach in the game. You can poke zombies without ever getting in their range.

Weapon Durability ?Don't Get Caught Empty-Handed

Weapons break. When they do, you're fighting with your fists, and that goes poorly:

  • Check the durability bar on your weapon icon. When it turns red, find a replacement before it snaps.
  • Maintenance skill slows durability loss. Even a couple levels makes a noticeable difference.
  • Whetstones sharpen bladed weapons ?right-click the weapon and select Sharpen.
  • Duct tape repairs makeshift weapons like reinforced bats and spears.

Always carry a backup weapon. Your main breaking mid-fight with nothing in slot two is how most runs end.

Quick Note on Guns

Firearms attract zombies from a massive radius. Don't use them unless you're surrounded and have no other option. If you do carry one, a handgun with 30?0 rounds of .38 or 9mm is plenty for emergency escapes. Save the hunting rifle and shotgun for situations where you absolutely need them.
