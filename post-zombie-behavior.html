How Zombies Detect You in Build 42 ?Sound, Sight, and Horde Mechanics

Written for Build 42 2026-05-11 ~7 min read

Understanding how zombies find you matters more than any combat technique. If you know what triggers them and how they move, you can avoid most dangerous encounters without throwing a single punch.

What Zombies Use to Find You

Two senses: sound and sight. That's it.

How Sound Works

Zombies hear everything. Here's the breakdown from loudest to quietest:

  • Gunshots ?Attract zombies across the entire map tile. Don't fire unless you have no other choice.
  • Sprinting ?Footsteps carry two or three blocks. Holding Shift for a full sprint is louder than a light jog.
  • Smashing windows ?Loud enough to pull zombies from one or two blocks away.
  • Banging on walls ?A technique for luring zombies away. Sound reaches roughly one block.
  • Walking ?Only audible to zombies in adjacent tiles. Much safer.
  • Sneaking ?Nearly silent. Crouching zombies will barely register your footsteps.

Important: sound travels through walls. Boarded windows don't block sound. Zombies outside can hear you moving around inside just fine. Boarding windows only blocks line of sight, not noise.

Line of Sight Rules

Zombies detect movement within their visual range, but there are nuances:

  • They see movement, not stationary targets ?If you stand perfectly still, a zombie can walk right past you without noticing.
  • Sheets and curtains block sight ?Cover your windows and zombies can't see you inside, even with lights on.
  • Light makes you visible at night ?A lit room with uncovered windows is basically a beacon. Cover them.
  • Without triggers, zombies wander randomly ?No sound, no sight = aimless movement.

The golden rule: if you can see a zombie through a window, it can see you. Cover every ground-floor window the moment you enter a building.

How Hordes Form

A horde isn't a pre-existing group wandering around. It's a gathering event triggered by sustained noise:

  1. Trigger: A gunshot, chainsaw, or mass zombie kills create a loud noise event.
  2. Gathering: Zombies from surrounding blocks all converge on the sound source.
  3. Dispersal: If they find nothing, they gradually wander off ?but many will linger nearby.
  4. Re-triggering: Make another loud noise while they're still in the area, and they all come back.

Never fight a horde. Even ten zombies will overwhelm you. If you accidentally pull one together, leave the area immediately. Don't come back until they've fully dispersed.

When to Sneak, When to Fight, When to Run

  • Sneak and avoid ?Default strategy for most encounters, especially in unfamiliar areas or low light. Lowest risk.
  • Melee combat ?1? isolated zombies, and only if you have a clear escape route behind you.
  • Ranged weapons ?Last resort when you're surrounded and can't break out. Remember: gunfire attracts more.
  • Run ?4+ zombies or a triggered horde. If your stamina is above 50%, just leave.

Zombie Behavior in Different Conditions

  • Winter ?Zombies move slower in freezing temperatures. Best time for outdoor supply runs.
  • Rain ?Ambient rain noise masks your footsteps slightly, making stealth more effective.
  • Night ?Zombies don't sleep. They wander at night just like during the day, but your visibility is worse, so accidental encounters are more likely.

Practical Tips That Save Runs

  • Bang on walls to redirect zombies ?Right-click a wall and select "Bang on Wall." Pulls nearby zombies toward the sound so you can slip past on the other side.
  • Closed doors block line of sight ?Enter a room, shut the door behind you, and zombies can't see you anymore.
  • Second floors are safer than first ?Zombies don't climb ladders. They do use stairs, but removing a staircase makes a room zombie-proof.
  • 80% stealth, 20% combat ?Your goal isn't to kill zombies. It's to survive. Avoid fights you don't need to take.
