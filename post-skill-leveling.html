Which Skills to Level First in Build 42 ?Fastest Training Methods

Written for Build 42 2026-05-11 ~7 min read

You can't level everything at once. Time is short, and spending it on the wrong skill means you'll struggle when it matters. Here's the priority order and how to train each one efficiently.

First Batch ?Survival Essentials

Fitness and Strength

These two are your foundation. Strength determines how much you can carry and how hard your melee hits land. Fitness controls your stamina pool and recovery speed.

How to train:

  • Right-click yourself and select Exercise. Do push-ups, sit-ups, and squats every morning for 10 minutes.
  • Walk around with a full backpack. Carrying weight passively builds Strength XP.
  • Use heavy weapons in combat ?axes and sledgehammers give more Strength XP than light ones.

Sprinting

If you can't run away, you die. Higher Sprinting means less stamina drain and faster movement. This skill literally saves your life.

How to train:

  • Sprint everywhere ?even on safe routes. The XP adds up quickly.
  • Sprint while carrying weight for bonus XP, but watch your stamina bar. Don't drain it dry before a fight.

Sneak and Lightfooted

Sneak makes your footsteps quieter ?zombies are less likely to notice you. Lightfooted prevents noise from stepping on broken glass, twigs, and alarm traps. Together they make you nearly invisible on foot.

How to train:

  • Hold Ctrl to crouch-walk past zombies. If they don't detect you, you gain XP.
  • Walk around your base while crouched. Stepping on carpets and grass is quiet enough to train without risk.

First Aid

Low First Aid means you can only slap on a basic bandage. Higher levels let you disinfect properly, stitch deep wounds, and speed up healing.

How to train:

  • Treat every wound yourself. Bandage scratches, apply disinfectant. Each treatment gives XP.
  • Read First Aid skill books. Each one gives a massive XP boost and unlocks new treatment options.

Second Batch ?Base Building Skills

Carpentry (Woodwork)

Boarding windows, building walls, crafting furniture, setting traps ?all Carpentry. Low levels let you nail planks; higher levels unlock walls, stairs, and metalworking structures.

How to train:

  • Board up every window you see. Each nailed window gives XP.
  • Build wooden crates and shelves ?they give significant XP and double as storage.
  • Read Carpentry skill books before practicing. Books give instant XP that multiplies your hands-on training.

Maintenance

This skill affects how long your weapons and tools last. Higher Maintenance = slower durability loss and faster repairs.

How to train:

  • Repair weapons with duct tape and sharpen blades with whetstones. Both actions give XP.
  • Fix damaged fences, doors, and barricades around your base. Repairing counts toward Maintenance.

Level These Later

Farming

Necessary for long-term food production, but not urgent. Canned food covers your first couple weeks easily.

How to train: Read Farming skill books first ?they give huge XP. Then plant and harvest. Each crop cycle levels you up.

Cooking

Higher Cooking means better nutrition and lower food poisoning chance.

How to train: Just cook every meal. It levels naturally as you eat.

Electrical and Metalworking

Generators, metal walls, and advanced equipment require these skills. They're mid-to-late game ?you won't need them in week one.

How to train: Primarily through skill books. Books are the main XP source. Read first, then practice on generators or metal structures.

Mechanics

Build 42's vehicle system is overhauled. Fixing and maintaining cars requires Mechanics. But if you don't have a working car yet, there's no point grinding this.

How to train: Find a broken vehicle and work on it. Read Mechanics skill books first for the XP boost.

XP Multipliers You Should Know About

  • Well Fed bonus: Being well-fed gives roughly a 20% XP boost to all skills. Eat before training sessions.
  • Happy bonus: High mood increases XP gain. Drink, read magazines, listen to the radio ?keep your character in a good headspace.
  • Read books before practicing: Skill books give a large one-time XP chunk. Read the book, then practice the skill. The order matters.
  • Focus on one skill at a time: Spreading XP across five skills means none of them reach a usable level. Push one skill to a functional point, then move to the next.

Daily routine suggestion: wake up, do 10 minutes of exercise (Fitness/Strength), then head out on a loot run (Sprinting/Sneak). Your most important skills level naturally without any dedicated grinding.

This guide was put together by players who've died to learn this stuff so you don't have to. Updated as the game changes.
