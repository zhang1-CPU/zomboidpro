Build 42 Season Survival ?What to Do Each Season and How to Prepare

Written for Build 42 2026-05-11 ~7 min read

Seasons aren't cosmetic in this game. Temperature, rain, and snow directly affect whether you live or die. Each season runs about 35 in-game days by default. What you need to do changes drastically from one to the next.

Spring ?Plant and Repair

The easiest season. Temperatures warm up, rain is frequent, and most importantly, you can farm again.

What to Do

  • Start planting immediately ?Potatoes, cabbage, and tomatoes grow well in spring. Right-click tilled soil ?Plant. Water daily.
  • Scout newly accessible areas ?Snow melt opens up buildings and routes you couldn't reach in winter.
  • Repair your base ?Fix winter-damaged boards, walls, and fences while the weather is good.
  • Stockpile seeds ?Hit garden sheds and hardware stores. More seeds now means more food later.

Watch Out For

Frequent rain means you'll get wet, and getting wet leads to colds. Keep a dry jacket in your inventory and swap it out the moment yours is soaked. Morning-to-evening temperature swings are real ?layer your clothing so you can strip down or bundle up as needed.

Summer ?Manage Water, Avoid Overheating

Rain is rare. Water becomes your most critical resource. Crops grow fast but need daily watering or they wilt.

What to Do

  • Fill every container before summer hits ?Rain barrels, pots, bottles, anything that holds water. Do this in late spring while it's still raining.
  • Water crops every single day ?One missed day and your plants start dying. Use a watering can or water bottles.
  • Switch to light clothing ?Shorts and t-shirts. Heavy clothes cause overheating, which drains stamina and increases thirst.
  • Preserve surplus food ?Make jerky, can vegetables, seal cooked meals in jars. Don't let a good harvest go to waste.

Watch Out For

Carry at least two water bottles on every supply run. Summer dehydration is fast and brutal. A full water bottle weighs 1kg ?that's a small price for staying alive. Physical activity drains stamina faster in heat, so rest more often than you think you need to.

Autumn ?Stockpile Like Your Life Depends on It

Autumn is your last preparation window before winter shuts everything down.

What to Do

  • Harvest everything ?Pick every crop, even unripe ones. They're still usable for cooking.
  • Cut and chop firewood ?Minimum 20?0 logs. Chop them into firewood with an axe. This is your winter heating fuel.
  • Preserve all excess food ?Jerky, pickling, canning. You can't grow anything in winter without a greenhouse.
  • Fortify your base ?Board up windows, reinforce doors. Winter is an indoor season.
  • Collect warm clothing ?Coats, boots, hats, gloves. Hit residential houses and clothing stores. You'll need all of it.

Watch Out For

Days get shorter. Plan your schedule around sunrise and sunset. Do your looting during daylight, save indoor tasks for nighttime. Catching a cold in late autumn is dangerous ?it can turn into something much worse once winter hits.

Winter ?Hunker Down

The hardest season. Freezing temperatures kill, snow slows movement by half, and farming is impossible without a heated greenhouse.

What to Do

  • Stay warm ?Keep your fireplace or stove burning. A warm base is the difference between thriving and freezing.
  • Ration firewood ?Don't burn it all in the first week. Use it sparingly, just enough to keep the cold out.
  • Don't go outside unless necessary ?Only venture out for food or firewood emergencies.
  • Ration food ?Calculate your supplies and stick to a schedule. Don't eat extra just because you're bored.
  • Watch your water ?Rain collectors freeze. Store water before the first snow, or melt snow in a pot over fire (costs firewood).

Watch Out For

Hypothermia starts with shivering and increasing fatigue, then progresses to health loss. Never go outside without full cold gear: coat, hat, gloves, and boots. Missing even one piece matters.

Snow reduces movement speed by 30?0% and drains stamina faster. Plan short trips with a clear route. Go out, get what you need, come back. Don't wander.
